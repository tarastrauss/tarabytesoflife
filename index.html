About Me


I'm a Los Angeles based web developer from New Jersey who first learned to code by creating a calculator in Visual Basic with my dad when I was 10-years-old. After coding in high school a bit, I took a break from programming to attend USC's School of Cinematic Arts. I worked in the entertainment industry for about 10 years, starting as an assistant on Battlestar Galactica. Most recently, I worked as a Creative Executive at a company that produces horror films. I rejuvenated my passion for programming this fall at General Assembly's Web Development Immersive and fell in love with coding all over again. I have an unhealthy love for DC superheroes, boardgames and a passion for cooking. Watch out - I can now pun in 4 languages!




Projects


Chef's Hat

Chef's Hat is an app geared towards helping users share their secret family recipes with their friends and family. The app was created using Ruby on Rails, PostgreSQL and Active-Record. Emphasis was put on user authentication and authorization - don't let those family secrets get out to the public!



ConShareto

ConShareto is a concert-tracking website to help the avid music-lover find and keep track of concerts. This was a group project built by myself and 4 other students from my cohort at General Assembly. The app uses Node.js, Express, MongoDB, the Jambase API and Google Oauth. My role in the project was primarily working with MongoDB schemas, controllers as well as implementing Google Oauth.



2048 Crisis

2048 Crisis is the very first web project I ever made. It used HTML, CSS, javasrscipt and jQuery. As a huge fan of both puzzles and DC superheroes, I decided to put my two passions together and make a themed game-clone of 2048. My emphasis went to flexing my javascript muscles and writing the game logic from scratch.
